Mastering "in case" and "just in case"

Quick Tip

In case: Used for precautions against something that might happen. It's about being prepared for a possibility. (e.g., "Take an umbrella in case it rains.")

Just in case: Often used more informally at the end of a sentence. It implies a smaller, less likely possibility, or a more general precaution. (e.g., "I'll take a snack, just in case.")
